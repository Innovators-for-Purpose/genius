This model shows a polaroid camera, which was first invented by Edwin Land, as part of the Polaroid Corporation.
This breakthrough in technology was created because of his daughter's demand to see an image taken of her instantly.
It was created by Land figuring out how to bring the exposed negative and positive paper through rollers. When the cameraâ€™s button was clicked, the shutter on the camera opened, which allowed light to enter it, hitting the photosensitive film, and recreating the image in front of the camera.
This technology cut the 2 hour wait to develop images that had been the norm and set the stage for instant hand held cameras and eventually smartphone cameras to make photographing easy and accessible.
